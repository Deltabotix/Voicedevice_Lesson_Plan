Lesson 7 — Microphone + speaker
===============================

Step 1: Understand it
---------------------

How does it work?

You **record** a few seconds from the mic (**arecord** → WAV file), check a simple
**loudness** number (peak 0–1), then **play** the clip back (**aplay**). That proves
both **listen** and **talk** paths on one Pi.

What will you need to do?

1. Set **INPUT_DEVICE**, **OUTPUT_DEVICE**, **RECORD_SECONDS**.
2. Record → loudness_level → play_sound in `my_program()`.
3. Run `./run 7`.

Real-world use

Voice assistants record your speech before sending it anywhere — same idea at a smaller scale.


Step 2: Create it
-----------------

Step A — settings

  INPUT_DEVICE = ""
  OUTPUT_DEVICE = ""
  RECORD_SECONDS = 3

Step B — my_program()

  check_audio_tools()
  record(RECORD_SECONDS, CAPTURE_FILE, INPUT_DEVICE)
  loudness_level(CAPTURE_FILE)
  play_sound(CAPTURE_FILE, OUTPUT_DEVICE)

Step C — run

  cd ~/lessons
  ./run 7

Speak during the 3-second record. Peak should not stay at 0.


Step 3: Test and improve it
---------------------------

Success

  • capture.wav appears.
  • Loudness peak > 0 when you talked.
  • You hear playback (volume low to avoid squeal).

Troubleshooting

  • arecord -l / aplay -l for device names.
  • Wrong INPUT_DEVICE → silent capture.
  • Feedback → lower speaker volume; use find_reply_sound() instead of replay.

Reset

  cd ~/lessons
  ./reset 7
